FANDOM HISTORY (2014 — Present)

2014

The Birth of a Fandom — August 8, 2014

FNaF 1 releases on Desura (later Steam). Markiplier plays the game on August 13, 2014, and his genuine terror reaction goes viral. The community forms around Game Theory's first FNAF analysis. The FNAF Wiki is founded by AdventureKid. Reddit's r/fivenightsatfreddys is created. Initial fan art appears on DeviantArt. The first fan songs are created.

2014-15

The Golden Age

FNaF 2 (Nov 2014), FNaF 3 (Mar 2015), FNaF 4 (Jul 2015) release in rapid succession. The community grows exponentially. Theory culture explodes. Fan art and fangame creation reach peak velocity. The Living Tombstone's "Five Nights at Freddy's" becomes a viral hit. FNAF merchandise begins production. The FNAF Wiki grows to hundreds of pages. The Fanon Wiki is established.

2016-17

Expansion and Division

Sister Location (2016) and Pizzeria Simulator (2017) expand the lore significantly. The MikeTrap vs WillTrap debate divides the community. FNaF World releases and is poorly received, then re-released as a free update. Scott Cawthon becomes more communicative with the community. The first FNAF novel trilogy concludes.

2018-20

The Modern Era

Ultimate Custom Night (2018) celebrates the series. Help Wanted (2019) brings FNAF to VR and reboots the lore. The Fanverse Initiative (2020) officially supports fangame developers. Security Breach is announced. Scott Cawthon retires from game development, passing the torch to Steel Wool Studios. The movie is officially announced.

2021-23

Mainstream Breakthrough

Security Breach (2021) brings FNAF to a mainstream audience. The FNAF Movie (2023) premieres worldwide, grossing nearly $300 million. Community viewing events, mass cosplay, and mainstream recognition. Help Wanted 2 (2023) expands the digital lore. The community reaches its peak popularity.

2024-25

The Future

10 Year Anniversary celebrations in 2024. Secret of the Mimic expands the lore. The FNAF 2 movie is announced. The community continues to thrive with new theories, fan content, and events. The FNAF Wiki hosts 863 pages and 25,848 files. The Fanon Wiki has 320 pages and 5,077 files. Unofficial FNAF Timeline projects and continued fangame development keep the community active.

MAJOR YOUTUBERS & CONTENT CREATORS

LEGEND

Markiplier (Mark Fischbach)

The single most important figure in FNAF's early popularity. His FNaF 1 gameplay from August 2014 has over 100 million views. His genuine reaction to Foxy's jumpscare became legendary. He played every mainline FNAF game, often with collaborator friends (Wade, Bob, Tyler). His influence made FNAF a mainstream indie horror phenomenon. Markiplier's FNAF content spans 10+ years of the series.

LEGEND

MatPat (Game Theory / The Game Theorists)

The most influential FNAF theorist. MatPat's Game Theory videos shaped how the community understood FNAF lore. Over 100 videos analyzing FNAF, from the first game to Security Breach. His theories on Purple Guy, the timeline, and the dream theory defined community discourse. His interview with Scott Cawthon was a landmark moment. The "MatPat effect" meant his theories influenced both the community and the games themselves.

LEGEND

Dawko (Lewis Dawkins)

British YouTuber dedicated almost exclusively to FNAF content. Known for: completing every FNAF game at maximum difficulty, charity streams (raising over $300,000 for mental health), the landmark 2-hour interview with Scott Cawthon in 2018, and FNAF song creation. Dawko is considered the bridge between the community and Scott Cawthon. His positivity and dedication define the best of the fandom.

CREATOR

FuhNaff

FNAF animator and content creator known for high-quality fan animations and lore breakdowns. Creator of the "FNAF: The Animated Series" and "The Man Behind the Slaughter" animations. Known for deep lore analysis and theorizing. Active in both the animation and theory communities.

CREATOR

FusionZGamer

One of the first major FNAF YouTubers in the Spanish-speaking community. Complete gameplay walkthroughs, theory videos, and community content. Helped build the Spanish-language FNAF community.

CREATOR

Razzbowski

Longtime FNAF content creator. Known for gameplay, challenge runs, and community interactions. Consistently covers every FNAF release since 2014.

CREATOR

8-BitRyan

Popular gameplay commentator known for FNAF playthroughs. Comedic commentary style. Covers all main games and popular fangames.

CREATOR

CoryxKenshin

Major gameplay YouTuber whose FNAF videos have tens of millions of views. Known for his energetic reactions and comedic style. Introduced FNAF to a broader audience.

CREATOR

The Amazing Atheist / Other Reactors

FNAF reaction videos became a genre of their own. Hundreds of YouTubers gained popularity through FNAF reactions, walkthroughs, and theory discussions.

FNAF MUSIC & MUSICIANS

PIONEER

The Living Tombstone

Yoav Landau created the definitive FNAF anthem. "Five Nights at Freddy's" (2014): 300M+ views, the first major FNAF fan song. "It's Been So Long" (FNaF 2 tribute): 150M+ views. "Die in a Fire" (FNaF 3): 120M+ views. TLT's electronic/pop style defined the FNAF music genre. His songs are widely considered the unofficial soundtrack of the fandom.

RAP

JT Music

John (JT) and his team created rapid FNAF songs for every game release. Hits include "Join Us for a Bite," "It's Me," and "Another Five Nights." Known for lore-accurate lyrics, clever wordplay, and energetic rap delivery. Their "FNAF Rap" series became a community staple.

POP

CG5 (Charlie Green)

Modern FNAF musician known for emotional pop-style songs. Major hits: "I Can't Fix You" (Sister Location), "You Can't Hide" (Help Wanted), "Break My Mind" (Security Breach). Known for poignant lyrics that explore the emotional core of FNAF characters. His music videos feature original animation.

METAL

DAGames (Will Ryan)

Rock/metal FNAF musician. "Break My Mind" became a Security Breach anthem. "I Will Not Be Moved" for Help Wanted. Known for powerful vocals, guitar-driven production, and epic choruses.

NARRATIVE

TryHardNinja

Creator of narrative-driven FNAF songs. "Just Gold" explores Golden Freddy's story. "Follow Me" tells William Afton's final moments. "Salvaged" narrates the FFPS fire. Known for storytelling through music.

MUSICAL

Random Encounters — FNAF: The Musical

Full live-action musical productions. Costumes, sets, original songs, and comedy. Multiple videos covering the FNAF story. Known for campy humor and fan-service casting. Became a cultural touchstone in the fandom.

COMMUNITY

FNAF Song Culture

Thousands of FNAF songs exist across YouTube, SoundCloud, and Spotify. The FNAF music community includes: Griffinilla, MandoPony, NateWantsToBattle, Madame Macabre, ShadyVox, and hundreds of independent artists. FNAF songs are a distinct genre of video game music fandom with annual "best of" compilations. Many songs become associated with specific characters (e.g., "I Can't Fix You" with Baby, "Just Gold" with Golden Freddy).

FAN ART COMMUNITY

PLATFORMS

DeviantArt, Tumblr, Twitter, Instagram, Reddit

FNAF fan art spans every major art platform. The fandom has produced millions of illustrations, from realistic portraits of Springtrap to cute chibi versions of Glamrock Freddy. The Human AU subgenre is one of the largest fan art movements. Daily uploads across all platforms maintain a constant stream of fresh content.

STYLES

Popular Fan Art Styles

Realistic horror (detailed, gory interpretations), Cartoon/chibi (cute versions of animatronics), Human AU (humanized characters), SFM renders (Source Filmmaker 3D scenes), Pixel art (retro game style), Watercolor/gothic (artistic interpretations), Crossover art (FNAF x other franchises), and Animation (2D and 3D fan animations).

MOST DRAWN

Most Popular Subjects

Springtrap (most drawn character), Circus Baby, Glamrock Freddy, Mangle, The Puppet, Golden Freddy, Foxy, Bonnie, and original OCs. Fan events like "Inktober" and "FNAF Art Month" generate themed content annually.

COSPLAY COMMUNITY

PROPS

Animatronic Cosplay

Cosplayers create elaborate animatronic suits using foam, EVA foam, 3D printing, LEDs, and mechanical parts. Springtrap cosplays are the most common due to his iconic design. Glamrock Freddy requires massive builds. Mangle cosplays challenge builders with their asymmetrical design. Animatronic cosplay has its own subculture with tutorials, patterns, and conventions dedicated to the craft.

HUMAN

Human AU Cosplay

Cosplayers dress as human versions of animatronics. These designs are often based on popular Human AU fan art. Requires costumes rather than animatronic builds, making them more accessible. Popular at conventions worldwide.

EVENTS

FNAF Cosplay Events

Major conventions (Comic-Con, PAX, MagFest, MomoCon) feature FNAF cosplay meetups. The FNAF Movie premiere (2023) saw the largest real-world gathering of FNAF cosplayers. Online cosplay competitions are held regularly on social media.

THE FNAF MOVIE FANDOM

PREMIERE

The FNAF Movie (October 27, 2023)

Directed by Emma Tammi. Starring Josh Hutcherson as Mike Schmidt, Matthew Lillard as William Afton, and Elizabeth Lail as Vanessa. The film grossed over $295 million worldwide against a $25 million budget. Simultaneous release in theaters and on Peacock. Community viewing parties worldwide. Massive cosplay turnout at premieres. The movie was a cultural event that brought FNAF to mainstream audiences.

CONTROVERSY

Movie Debates

Community discussions: faithfulness to game lore (the movie has a simplified timeline), casting choices (Matthew Lillard as Afton was universally praised), the PG-13 rating (some wanted R-rated horror), and whether the film should have followed game events more closely. The post-credit scene teased the FNaF 2 movie.

SEQUEL

FNAF 2 Movie (Upcoming)

Announced in 2024. Expected to adapt FNaF 2's story with the Toy animatronics and the Puppet. Fan casting debates continue. The sequel will likely address the first film's unanswered questions and expand the cinematic universe.

FNAF WIKI COMMUNITY STATS

MAIN WIKI

Freddy Fazbear's Pizza Wiki (Fandom)

Founded: 2014 by AdventureKid. Pages: 863. Files: 25,848. Editors: Active community of volunteers. The most comprehensive source of FNAF information in Spanish. Covers: games, characters, locations, books, movies, fangames, and theories. Daily updates ensure the wiki remains current with each new game and theory.

FANON WIKI

FNAF Fanon Wiki (Fandom)

Founded: 2014. Pages: 320. Files: 5,077. Dedicated to fan-created content: original characters, fan stories, alternate universes, fan songs, and roleplay worlds. The fanon community maintains separate guidelines and categories. Spanish-language content is a significant portion.

COMMUNITY

Wiki Community Features

Discussion forums, blog posts, user profiles, contests, and community projects. The wiki runs regular events: article of the month, best OC contest, collaborative lore projects. Moderation team ensures quality standards. Cross-wiki collaboration between main and fanon wikis.

COMMUNITY PLATFORMS & EVENTS

REDDIT

r/fivenightsatfreddys

The largest FNAF subreddit. 800,000+ members. Hub for theories, fan art, news, and discussions. Known for its detailed theory posts, lore analysis, and community collaborations. Subreddit events include "Theory Thursday" and "Fanart Friday."

DISCORD

FNAF Discord Servers

Major servers: Freddit (largest), Security Breach Server, FNAF Wiki Server, and dozens of fan game servers. Real-time lore discussion, voice chats, events, and community games. Some servers host competitive FNAF tournaments.

TWITTER

FNAF on Twitter/X

Active community of artists and theorists. Key hashtags: #FNAF, #FNAFArt, #FNAFCommunity, #FNAFMovie, #FNAFTheory. Official accounts: @SteelWoolStudio, @FNAFMovie. Fan accounts with large followings share daily content.

EVENTS

Major Community Events

10 Year Anniversary (2024): global celebration with streams, art, and retrospectives. Fanverse Initiative Announcement (2020): historic support for fangames. FNAF Movie Premiere (2023): largest real-world gathering. Dawko's Charity Streams: raised $300K+ for mental health. Weekly theory discussion threads on Reddit.

CONVENTIONS

Convention Presence

FNAF has a major presence at: PAX East/West (panels, cosplay), San Diego Comic-Con (panels, merch, movie events), MagFest (FNAF tournament), MomoCon (animation focus), Minecon (crossover events), and local conventions worldwide. FNAF cosplay meetups are a staple at any major gaming convention.

MEMES & FANDOM CULTURE

ICONIC

"The Bite of '87"

The most referenced meme in FNAF history. Every new game release prompts "Is this the Bite of '87?" jokes. The original FNaF 1 phone call mentioning the bite became legendary. The FNaF 4 bite reveal created years of debate. Still used as a community in-joke.

ICONIC

"He's Just Standing There... MENACINGLY!"

Refers to animatronics appearing in doorways without moving. Originated from early FNAF gameplay where Freddy's silhouette blocks the door. Captures the eerie stillness of the animatronics.

ICONIC

"Is That the Bite of '87?"

Markiplier's quote from his FNaF 4 playthrough became a permanent community catchphrase. Used whenever any character gets bitten or injured.

ICONIC

"William Afton Did Nothing Wrong"

Ironic meme defending the franchise's villain. Often used to mock bad takes or to playfully argue about lore. Accompanied by "He was just trying to achieve immortality."

ICONIC

"Mangle is a Dog" vs "Mangle is a Fox"

Long-running debate about Mangle's species. Despite official materials calling Mangle a fox, the debate persists as a community running gag.

CULTURE

FNAF Meme Culture

Deep-fried memes, Gacha Life FNAF content, "FNAF Porn" (ironic and genuine), "FNAF Logic" videos, parody content, and reaction memes. The fandom's self-awareness about its own tropes (edgy OCs, Human AUs, ship art) is a defining feature of FNAF internet culture.
