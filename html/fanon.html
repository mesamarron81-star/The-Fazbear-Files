FANON ORIGINAL CHARACTERS (OCs)

Original animatronic characters created by the FNAF Fanon Wiki community.

OC

Lusania

A prominent fanon animatronic from the Spanish-speaking community. Original design with unique backstory involving a haunted pizzeria in an alternate timeline. Lusania appears in multiple fan stories and fan games. Characterized by a elegant, vintage animatronic design with thematic ties to 19th-century aesthetics. Documented extensively on the Fanon Wiki with multiple story appearances.

OC

Dark Bunny

Fanon interpretation of Bonnie as a dark, shadowy entity. Possesses supernatural powers and an edgy aesthetic. Popular in fan art and AU stories. Often paired with other shadow animatronics in fanon lore.

OC

Shadow Mangle

A fanon version of Mangle reimagined as a dark spirit entity. Extremely popular in fan art communities. Depicted as a more sinister and powerful version of the original Mangle, with corrupted design elements.

OC

Glitch Bonnie

A digital corruption version of Bonnie inspired by Help Wanted's Glitchtrap. Pixelated, glitching design. Represents a virus-infected animatronic in fanon digital horror stories.

OC

Fazbear OCs Collection

The Fanon Wiki hosts hundreds of original animatronic characters created by community members. These include: Candy the Cat (alternate versions), Springtrap variants, Freddy recolors, original hybrid animatronics, and entirely new concepts. Each OC has detailed wiki pages with backstory, abilities, and design specifications.

OC

Fanon Animatronic Types

Categories of fanon OCs documented include: Phantom variants (based on FNaF 3 hallucinations), Withered recolors (damaged versions), Toy remakes (FNaF 2-style), Glamrock variants (Security Breach-style), Melted animatronics, Corrupted entities, Humanized versions, and Original species with no canon counterpart.

FANON STORIES & SERIES

SAGA

The Return (Saga)

A major fanon story series exploring the return of original animatronics. Multiple chapters with interconnected plotlines. Features new locations, original characters, and expanded lore. One of the most comprehensive fanon narratives on the wiki, spanning multiple story arcs and character developments.

HOLIDAY

Una Navidad en Freddy's

Spanish-language fanon Christmas story. The animatronics celebrate Christmas in the abandoned restaurant. Bittersweet tones blending holiday cheer with the melancholic atmosphere of the FNAF universe. Includes original illustrations and community contributions.

PRECUEL

FNaF: Fredbear's Family End

Fanon prequel exploring the final days of Fredbear's Family Diner. Emotional narrative about the decline of the original location before the events of FNaF 4. Explores the first tragedies that led to the Freddy Fazbear's Pizza empire.

RPG

FNaF World: Different Universe

Extensive fanon expansion of the FNAF World RPG. New characters, worlds, abilities, and storylines. Functions as both a story series and a fangame concept. One of the most content-rich fanon projects on the wiki, with detailed worldbuilding and character rosters.

COMEDY

FNaF: Stupid Saga

Comedy fanon series. Light-hearted adventures of the animatronics in absurd situations. Popular for its humor and contrast to the dark tone of canonical FNAF. Multiple episodes/entries with recurring characters and running gags.

HORROR

Five Nights at Freddy's: Bloody Party

Fanon horror story about a birthday party that descends into chaos. Original characters and animatronics. Dark, survival-horror narrative. Series format with multiple installments documenting the bloody events of the party.

PSYCHOLOGICAL

AD: The Discarded Story

Psychological horror fanon narrative about a forgotten animatronic left in storage. Themes of abandonment, memory, and identity. Told from the perspective of the discarded animatronic. Considered one of the more emotionally impactful fanon stories.

INVESTIGATION

FNAF: El misterio del local

Spanish-language fanon investigation series. A group of teenage friends explore an abandoned Freddy Fazbear's Pizza. Combines mystery, horror, and teen drama. Popular in Spanish-speaking FNAF communities.

ALTERNATE UNIVERSES (AUs)

AU

Human AU (Animatronics as Humans)

The most popular fanon AU. Every animatronic has a human counterpart with matching personality traits. Freddy is a kind leader, Bonnie a rockstar, Chica a food-loving girl, Foxy a pirate enthusiast. Extremely popular in fan art, cosplay, and roleplay communities.

AU

FNAF High School AU

Alternate universe set in a high school. Characters are students, teachers, and staff. Drama, romance, and comedy elements. The animatronics attend classes, deal with teenage problems, and uncover mysteries.

AU

FNAF Monster AU

Supernatural alternate universe. Animatronics are reimagined as monsters: vampires, werewolves, ghosts, demons. Each character gets a monster form with unique powers. Darker tone than most AUs.

AU

FNAF Roleplay AU

Community-driven roleplay universes. Detailed character profiles, relationship charts, and storylines developed interactively by community members. Each RP AU has its own wiki page documenting the setting and rules.

AU

Cyberpunk FNAF AU

Futuristic AU where animatronics are cybernetic beings in a neon-lit city. Hacking, digital consciousness, and corporate espionage themes. Reimagines classic characters with cybernetic enhancements and cyberpunk aesthetics.

AU

Fantasy Kingdom AU

Medieval fantasy AU. Freddy is a king, Bonnie a knight, Chica a chef, Foxy a pirate captain, and so on. Set in a magical kingdom with castles, quests, and fantasy elements.

FAN SONGS & MUSIC

ICONIC

"Five Nights at Freddy's" — The Living Tombstone

The most iconic FNAF fan song. Over 300 million views on YouTube. Released 2014. Created by Yoav Landau (The Living Tombstone). The song captures the essence of the first game and launched the FNAF music phenomenon.

ICONIC

"It's Been So Long" — The Living Tombstone

Second major FNAF song by TLT. Connected to FNaF 2's lore. Emotional lyrics about the missing children. Over 150 million views.

MUSICIAN

JT Music

Rap duo known for FNAF-themed songs. Created tracks for every major game release. Popular entries: "Join Us for a Bite," "Die in a Fire," "It's Me." Known for energetic rap verses and lore-accurate lyrics.

MUSICIAN

CG5

Charlie "CG5" Green. Created numerous FNAF songs with catchy melodies. Popular songs: "I Can't Fix You" (FNAF: SL), "You Can't Hide" (FNAF: HW), "Break My Mind" (FNAF: SB). Known for emotional and melodic style.

MUSICIAN

Dawko

YouTuber and musician. Created FNAF tribute songs. Known for "FNAF Song" series and covers. Also famous for his charity streams and community events.

MUSICIAN

DAGames

Will "DAGames" Ryan. Created rock/metal FNAF songs. Popular tracks: "Break My Mind," "I Will Not Be Moved" (FNAF: HW). Known for powerful vocals and rock production.

MUSICIAN

TryHardNinja

Created multiple FNAF songs including "Just Gold," "Follow Me," and "Salvaged." Known for narrative-driven lyrics that tell FNAF stories through music.

MUSICAL

FNAF: The Musical (Random Encounters)

Full musical production by Random Encounters. Features live-action actors in animatronic costumes. Multiple videos telling the FNAF story through song and dance. Popular for its campy humor and catchy tunes.

COMMUNITY

FNAF Song Community on Fanon Wiki

The Fanon Wiki documents fan-created lyrics, song concepts, and musical projects. Includes original songs written by community members, parody lyrics, and fan-made music videos. Over 50 documented fan song projects on the wiki.

FANON COMMUNITY HISTORY

FOUNDING

FNAF Fanon Wiki — Founding (2014-2015)

The FNAF Fanon Wiki was established on Fandom (formerly Wikia) in late 2014 as a sister project to the main FNAF Wiki. Purpose: to host fan-created content separate from canonical information. Initial focus was on original characters and fan stories.

GROWTH

Expansion Era (2016-2018)

The wiki grew rapidly during the FNAF golden age. New content categories emerged: Alternate Universes, Fan Games concepts, Fan Songs, and Roleplay worlds. The community developed its own wiki guidelines, templates, and administrative structure. Over 200 articles were created during this period.

MODERN

Modern Era (2019-Present)

The Fanon Wiki continues to grow with new content inspired by modern FNAF games (Help Wanted, Security Breach). New character types based on Glamrock animatronics, digital entities, and the movie characters. The wiki now has 320+ pages and 5,077 files as of 2025.

CULTURE

Fanon Community Culture

The Fanon Wiki community is known for its creativity and inclusivity. Monthly contests for best OC, story, and artwork. Collaborative worldbuilding projects. Cross-wiki collaborations with the main FNAF Wiki. Spanish-language content is a significant portion of the wiki's articles.

FANON WIKI ADMINISTRATION

ADMIN

Wiki Administration & Bureaucrats

The FNAF Fanon Wiki is managed by a team of administrators and bureaucrats who oversee content, enforce guidelines, and manage community disputes. Key administrative roles: Bureaucrats (highest authority), Administrators (content management), Content Moderators (page quality), Discussion Moderators (community engagement).

GUIDELINES

Fanon Content Guidelines

The wiki maintains specific guidelines for fanon content: No explicit adult content, credit original creators, no plagiarism, follow wiki formatting standards, categorize pages properly (OCs, stories, AUs, songs, etc.), and maintain a respectful community environment.

CATEGORIES

Fanon Wiki Category Structure

Articles organized into categories: Characters (OCs, human AUs, monsters), Stories (series, one-shots, crossovers), Games (fanon game concepts), Songs (original lyrics, parodies), Locations (fanon restaurants, maps), Timelines (alternate event sequences), and Roleplay (character profiles, setting docs).
