CONFIRMED THEORIES

STATUS: VERIFIED — Supported by official confirmation or irrefutable in-game evidence.

CONFIRMED

Michael Afton is the Protagonist of FNaF 1, 3, 4, and Sister Location

Scott Cawthon confirmed that Michael Afton (son of William) is the night guard in the majority of the games. He uses the aliases Mike Schmidt (FNaF 1) and Fritz Smith (FNaF 2). His decaying body explains the hallucinations and his resistance to death. The custom night cutscene in Sister Location shows Michael speaking to his father. Evidence: SL cutscene dialog, HandUnit references, the Survival Logbook confirms Michael's identity.

CONFIRMED

William Afton is Springtrap

Officially confirmed in FNaF 3 and later games. William Afton hides in the Spring Bonnie suit during a chase, the springlocks fail, and he becomes trapped for 30 years. Springtrap is the decomposing corpse of William inside the suit. Evidence: FNaF 3 minigames, FNaF 3 ending, FNaF 6 (Pizzeria Simulator) confirms William's survival. The charred corpse is visible inside the suit across multiple games.

CONFIRMED

Charlotte Emily is The Puppet / Marionette

Confirmed via FNaF 2 minigames and Pizzeria Simulator. Henry Emily's daughter Charlotte was murdered outside Freddy Fazbear's Pizza by William Afton. Her soul possesses the Puppet, who then gives life to the other murdered children. Evidence: "Security Puppet" minigame (FFPS) shows Charlotte's death, "Give Gifts, Give Life" minigame (FNaF 2) shows the Puppet giving life. The novel trilogy also confirms this backstory.

CONFIRMED

Glitchtrap / Burntrap is William Afton's Digital Consciousness

The digital entity in Help Wanted (Glitchtrap) and the amalgamation in Security Breach (Burntrap) are William Afton's consciousness. His mind was scanned into the game code during the development of the VR game. Evidence: Help Wanted tapes reveal a developer scanned an artifact (Spring Bonnie fur), The Storyteller (Tales from the Pizzaplex) confirms this, Security Breach ending shows Burntrap controlling the Blob.

CONFIRMED

Cassidy is the Fifth Missing Child — Golden Freddy

The fifth missing child, named Cassidy, possesses Golden Freddy. Evidence: The Survival Logbook contains the name "Cassidy" hidden in word searches, the "Cassidy" name appears in Security Breach's files (the Princess Quest arcade cabinet), and the novels confirm a female child possessing Golden Freddy. The "IT'S ME" messages and the unique ghost-like abilities of Golden Freddy support this.

CONFIRMED

Henry Emily is the Founder of Fazbear Entertainment

Henry Emily co-founded Fredbear's Family Diner and later Fazbear Entertainment with William Afton. He designed the original animatronics, including the spring-lock suits. Confirmed in FNaF 6 (Pizzeria Simulator) insanity ending and the novel trilogy. He returns in FFPS to set fire to the labyrinth and end everything.

CONFIRMED

The Missing Children's Incident (MCI) of 1985

Five children were lured into a back room and murdered by William Afton at the original Freddy Fazbear's Pizza. Their bodies were hidden inside the animatronic suits. Evidence: Go! Go! Go! minigame (FNaF 2) shows Foxy watching children die, Give Gifts Give Life shows 5 bodies, the novels (The Silver Eyes) explicitly depict this event. The year 1985 is confirmed via the novels and game references.

PROBABLE THEORIES

STATUS: LIKELY — Strong evidence supports these theories, but official confirmation is pending.

PROBABLE

The Crying Child (Evan/BV) is Golden Freddy or Nightmare

The younger Afton son, bitten by Fredbear in FNaF 4, likely possesses an entity. Leading theories: he shares Golden Freddy with Cassidy (two souls theory), he becomes the source of Nightmare animatronics, or he becomes Shadow Freddy. Evidence: FNaF 4 hospital equipment, the "I will put you back together" speech, the Survival Logbook's "I am still here" message, parallel to the novel's Michael Brooks character.

PROBABLE

Elizabeth Afton is Circus Baby / Scrap Baby

William's daughter Elizabeth was killed by Circus Baby, designed specifically for her. Her soul now possesses Baby. Evidence: Sister Location reveals Baby speaks like a child (Elizabeth), the Scooper ending, FFPS confirms Scrap Baby is Elizabeth (her dialog references "Daddy"), the novels confirm this backstory. The only missing piece is a direct statement from Scott.

PROBABLE

The Bite of '87 was Caused by Mangle

The Bite of '87, mentioned in FNaF 1 and 2, was likely caused by Mangle. Evidence: FNaF 2 phone calls mention the animatronics can walk during the day, Mangle is disassembled and accessible to children (the "take apart Mangle" minigame), Toy Chica removes her beak (suggesting she could bite), but Mangle's position in the ceiling makes it the most likely candidate for a frontal lobe bite.

PROBABLE

Henry Created Lefty to Capture the Puppet

Lefty (Lure Encapsulate Fuse Transport Extract) was specifically designed by Henry to capture and contain Charlotte (the Puppet). Evidence: The name is an acronym, Lefty's blueprints in FFPS show a containment system, the Puppet is inside Lefty in FFPS, Henry's plan to "free" his daughter required bringing her to the fire.

PROBABLE

Order of the Missing Children's Incident

Based on minigame evidence and the "gravestone order" in FNaF 3, the order of possession is likely: Susie (Chica) → Jeremy (Bonnie) → Gabriel (Freddy) → Fritz (Foxy) → Cassidy (Golden Freddy). Evidence: Fruity Maze minigame (FFPS) shows Susie being lured first, the gravestone order in FNaF 3 ending, Charlies's death predates them all (she was killed first).

PROBABLE

The Nightmare Animatronics are Real (Experiment Theory)

Alternative to the dream theory: the Nightmare animatronics in FNaF 4 are real animatronics used in experiments by William Afton. Evidence: The "Nightmare" animatronic has a file texture named "experiment", Sister Location's private room has surveillance of the FNaF 4 house, the breaker room map shows the FNaF 4 house layout. This suggests William was testing fear responses.

DEBATED THEORIES

STATUS: CONTESTED — Active debate continues in the community. No consensus reached.

DEBATED

Dream Theory (Everything is a Dream)

The theory that all FNAF games are the dreams/nightmares of the Crying Child in his coma. Extremely popular in 2015. Scott Cawthon publicly debunked this theory in a Steam post, but it resurfaces in community discussions. Counter-argument: too many concrete events happen across games that would not be known to a child. Status: officially debunked but historically significant.

DEBATED

Is the Crying Child's Name Evan, Norman, or Something Else?

The name of the Crying Child (FNaF 4 protagonist) is unconfirmed. Common fan names include: Evan (from the "Evan" coffin in the logbook), Norman (from FNaF World files), Chris (popular fan name), or simply "The Crying Child" (BV/Bite Victim). The name "Evan" has the most evidence but remains debated.

DEBATED

Who is the Night Guard in FNaF 3?

Is the FNaF 3 guard Michael Afton seeking his father, or an unrelated employee? Evidence for Michael: the "Phantom" animatronics are hallucinations similar to FNaF 4, and Michael is the series protagonist. Evidence against: no direct confirmation, the guard simply works at Fazbear's Fright. Status: likely Michael, but not confirmed.

DEBATED

What is the Blob / Tangle in Security Breach?

The Blob (also called Tangle) is a massive amalgamation of endoskeletons and animatronic parts found below the Pizzaplex. Theories: it's the remains of the FFPS fire, it's controlled by Cassidy, it's an evolved version of Molten Freddy, or it's a new entity entirely. The blue eyes suggest a connection to the original missing children.

DEBATED

Is Vanessa/Vanny the Same Person?

Security Breach features Vanessa (security guard) and Vanny (rabbit killer). Are they the same person with a split personality (influenced by Glitchtrap), or different people? Evidence for same: Vanessa's appearance when unmasked, dialog references. Evidence against: different body types in some renders. The Tales from the Pizzaplex books suggest Vanessa was the first victim of Glitchtrap's mind control.

HISTORIC THEORIES

STATUS: LEGACY — These theories shaped the FNAF community and our understanding of the lore.

HISTORIC

Phone Guy is Purple Guy (2014)

One of the earliest FNAF theories. Based on Phone Guy's knowledge of the animatronics and his suspicious tone, the community believed Phone Guy was the killer. Debunked by FNaF 2 which shows Phone Guy dying in a call. But this theory kickstarted the entire lore-hunting culture of FNAF.

HISTORIC

Purple Guy Identity Crisis (2014-2015)

After FNaF 2 introduced multiple purple-colored characters, the community debated: is there one purple guy or two? The "Purple Guy" sprite varied in color between games. Eventually the community distinguished between William Afton (purple) and Michael Afton (pink/purple, rotting). This debate established the color-coding system used in lore analysis.

HISTORIC

Five Kids Theory (2014-2015)

The gradual discovery that there were five missing children, not four. Go! Go! Go! minigame and Give Gifts, Give Life revealed the truth. The fifth child (Golden Freddy) was discovered through the FNaF 1 poster and later the FNaF 2 minigames. This was a paradigm shift in understanding FNAF lore.

HISTORIC

Springlock Suit Theory (2015)

Before FNaF 3, the community theorized about hybrid suits that could function as both animatronics and wearable suits. Phone Guy's FNaF 3 tapes revealed the "spring lock" mechanism. The community correctly predicted the existence of a springlock failure incident and a suit that trapped the killer. FNaF 3 confirmed this with Springtrap.

HISTORIC

Sister Location Prediction Theory (2015-2016)

Based on the pink/white room in FNaF 4 and the "Private Room" mention, the community speculated about a "sister location" to Freddy Fazbear's Pizza. The teasers for Circus Baby and the key confirmed this. The community accurately predicted many elements of Sister Location before its release.

HISTORIC

Miketrap vs Willtrap Debate (2017)

The biggest debate in FNAF history. After Sister Location, the community split on whether Springtrap was Michael Afton (Miketrap) or William Afton (Willtrap). The debate lasted months, consumed thousands of posts. Scott eventually confirmed Willtrap via The Ultimate Guide book. This remains the most famous theory war in the fandom.

HISTORIC

Bite of '87 vs Bite of '83 (2015-2017)

The community argued for years whether the bite shown in FNaF 4 was the Bite of '87 (mentioned in FNaF 1) or an earlier event. Evidence: the TV shows "1983" in FNaF 4, the FNaF 1 phone call says "the Bite of '87" while FNaF 4 appears to be set earlier. Scott confirmed via a source code comment that it was 1983. The debate fundamentally changed how the community analyzed dates in the games.

DISCARDED THEORIES

STATUS: REFUTED — Proven wrong by official statements or contradictory evidence.

DISCARDED

Mike = Phone Guy = Purple Guy

Early 2014 theory that Mike Schmidt, Phone Guy, and the killer were all the same person. Discarded by FNaF 2 and 3 which show Phone Guy dying (heard on tape) and Purple Guy being a distinct character. Michael Afton and Phone Guy are now known to be different people.

DISCARDED

Foxy is a Good Animatronic

Based on the FNaF 3 minigame where Foxy appears to help and the "GO GO GO" minigame showing Foxy watching the murders. Some believed Foxy was trying to help the children. Discarded: Foxy is equally dangerous, attacks the guard, and is clearly hostile in all main games.

DISCARDED

The Novel Trilogy is the Same Timeline as the Games

Early fans believed The Silver Eyes, The Twisted Ones, and The Fourth Closet were direct canon. Scott clarified they are a separate continuity (book universe) that shares character names and general concepts but has a different timeline. Elements from the books can be used to understand the games, but they are not the same story.

DISCARDED

Golden Freddy is a Hallucination Only

Some theorized Golden Freddy was purely a hallucination with no real possessed animatronic. Discarded by multiple game appearances showing Golden Freddy is a physical entity (FNaF 1 custom night, FNaF 2 hallway, FNaF 3 minigames). He is now confirmed as Cassidy.

DISCARDED

The Bite of '87 was Caused by Freddy or Foxy

Early theories blamed Freddy or Foxy for the Bite of '87. Discarded by evidence pointing to Mangle (FNaF 2's most unstable animatronic, accessible to children, positioned in the ceiling). Foxy runs but doesn't bite. Freddy stays on stage.

THEORY TIMELINE

Major developments in the FNAF community's understanding of the lore.

2014

August — FNaF 1 Release

Initial theories focus on Phone Guy's identity, the missing children, and why the animatronics attack. Game Theory's first video analyzes the first game.

2014

November — FNaF 2 Release

Purple Guy minigames revealed. The "Give Gifts, Give Life" minigame shows the Puppet giving life. Five children confirmed. The concept of multiple timelines emerges.

2015

March — FNaF 3 Release

Springtrap revealed. Springlock suits confirmed. The Purple Guy dies in Spring Bonnie. Happiest Day minigame suggests a resolution. FNaF 3 post-release changes fuel speculation.

2015

July — FNaF 4 Release

Dream Theory dominates. "Bite of '83 vs '87" debate begins. Crying Child introduced. The community is divided for years.

2016

October — Sister Location Release

Michael Afton confirmed as protagonist. The MikeTrap vs WillTrap debate begins. Elizabeth Afton theory gains traction. Baby and the Funtimes revealed.

2017

FFPS (FNaF 6) releases. Henry Emily speaks. The fire ending concludes the original story. MikeTrap finally debunked. The lore is officially concluded (until Help Wanted).

2019

Help Wanted releases. Glitchtrap introduced. The game becomes canon. Digital consciousness theories emerge. The lore restarts with a new era.

2021

Security Breach releases. The Blob, Glamrock animatronics, and Gregory introduced. New era of theories about the Pizzaplex and Mimic.

2023

FNaF Movie releases. Help Wanted 2 expands digital lore. The Mimic confirmed as Security Breach's main antagonist via Tales books.

2025

Secret of the Mimic expands the lore further. The community continues to analyze and debate. The FNAF Wiki archives 863 pages of theories and analysis.
